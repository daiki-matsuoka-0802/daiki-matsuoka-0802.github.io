Daiki Matsuoka

PhD student at the Department of Computer Science, The University of Tokyo (he/him)

I work in the field of formal semantics of natural language. In other words, I aim to develop a mathematical model that explains humans’ cognitive ability to compute the meaning of natural language expressions.

More concretely, my research focuses on the following topics.

  • Type-theoretical semantics
    • Type theory, a formal system developed in computer science, serves as a powerful tool for analyzing meanings (see my slides here for details).
  • Categorial grammar
    • In this framework, syntactic structures are built together with meaning representations, providing a computationally plausible model of meaning composition.

I am currently a member of Yanaka Lab at the University of Tokyo.

I have another website (in Japanese), where I occasionally post some notes on what I have self-studied (partly as a hobby).
